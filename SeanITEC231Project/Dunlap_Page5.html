Nowadays, especially in these trying times of 2020, people want thier privacy, just as much as they want their freedom of speech. But when does censorship go to far legally?

As stated on the "Morals of the United States" page, the US has a duty to protect it's citizens.

HOWEVER, in more recent years, there has been a shift in governing the free speech. The shift is going from traditional speech, to social media

Therefore, the government is delegating authority to private actors (such as Facebook) to determine what is legally allowed on their social media sites. But, is this legal?
